Subject: Request for name support for the Mitsue Project

Dear Ms. Pellegrom, dear Sandra,

With reference to our conversation during King's Day on April 24th and our subsequent correspondence, I am writing to formally ask whether you would consider lending your name to the Mitsue Project as a gesture of support.

The Mitsue Project is a 25-year initiative to transform a closed village school in Mitsue, Nara Prefecture, into an integrated center for sustainable development, combining native forest restoration, locally generated clean energy, and small-scale digital infrastructure. The project works in close partnership with the local village government, private forest landowners, and the Mitsue community, and is advised by Joi Ito (former Director, MIT Media Lab) and Ray Ozzie (former Chief Software Architect, Microsoft), among others.

The project aligns closely with the sustainable development goals you have championed throughout your career — in particular SDG 17, partnerships for the goals — and offers a concrete example of how Dutch expertise in sustainability and the circular economy can contribute to rural revitalization in Japan.

This request is not for financial or formal commitment from the Consulate-General or from you personally. What I am asking is solely that we be permitted to list your name — preferably in your capacity as Consul-General — as a supporter of the project in our documentation and on our website. This gesture would make the Dutch-Japanese dimension of the initiative visible and would be of significant value in our conversations with Japanese partners and potential funders.

Specifically, your name support would entail:
- Listing on the project website and in project documentation as "with support from" (exact wording subject to your approval)
- No financial commitment, no executive role, no formal liability
- Optional invitation to receive project updates, at your preference
- The ability to withdraw your support at any time, without conditions

Should you wish to provide this form of support, I would welcome the opportunity to coordinate with you on the exact wording and on any protocol considerations vis-à-vis The Hague. If a different level of engagement seems more appropriate to you — whether more formal or more informal — I am of course open to that as well.

I fully understand that this request requires time and internal coordination, and there is no urgency whatsoever. Should you be unable or unwilling to honor the request, my gratitude remains undiminished for the open exchange of ideas during King's Day and for your interest in the project.

## Notes on Sending the Letter

1. **Send the Dutch version as primary.** Sandra is Dutch and the relationship is Dutch-to-Dutch. Including the English is courteous but secondary.

2. **Format as PDF, attached to a brief covering email.** A formal letter sent only as email body looks less considered than a formatted PDF that she can save or share.

3. **The covering email should be very brief**, something like:

   > "Beste Sandra,
   >
   > Bijgaand vind je, zoals besproken, een formeel verzoek met betrekking tot het Mitsue Project, evenals het uitgebreidere projectoverzicht.
   >
   > Neem alle tijd die je nodig hebt — er is geen haast bij.
   >
   > Met vriendelijke groet,
   > Rob"

4. **Do not follow up for at least 4 weeks.** Diplomatic correspondence moves slowly and consulates have annual cycles. Patience reads as professionalism.

5. **If she agrees, request her preferred wording in writing.** Diplomats are very particular about how their name and title appear; never assume — always ask.

6. **Keep the original PDF on file.** It may need to be referenced months later when other partners ask about the consulate's involvement.
