# Founder Agreement Template
### Mitsue Project — Founding Members
**Draft for discussion — not a final legal document**

---

## Important Note

This is a **template for an early-stage, informal founder agreement** designed to align expectations between founding members **before** formal NPO/Ippan Shadan Hojin incorporation. Once the legal entity is established, formal bylaws (定款) will supersede this document.

**This is not a substitute for legal review.** Before any founder signs, both parties should review with a Japanese lawyer or 行政書士. This template is meant to surface and resolve key questions early — not to replace formal legal documentation.

---

### Article 1 — Purpose and Mission

### Article 2 — Roles and Responsibilities

| Founder C | [e.g. Forestry Liaison] | [hours/week] | [Volunteer / stipend / TBD] |

Founders agree to communicate proactively if their availability or commitment changes.

### Article 3 — Decision-Making

Until formal incorporation, decisions of the founding group shall be made as follows:

- **Routine decisions** (operational, day-to-day): may be made by the relevant Founder in their area of responsibility.
- **Significant decisions** (financial commitments above ¥100,000, public communications, partnerships, hiring): require consensus among all Founders.
- **Major decisions** (mission changes, founder additions or departures, legal incorporation): require unanimous written agreement.

If consensus cannot be reached, Founders agree to seek mediation from a mutually trusted advisor before any unilateral action.

### Article 4 — Intellectual Property

- Any intellectual property developed by Founders specifically for the Project shall be owned by the Project (once incorporated) or held collectively by the Founders (until incorporation).
- Any intellectual property a Founder brings to the Project from prior work remains their property; they grant the Project a perpetual, royalty-free license to use it for Project purposes.
- All technical knowledge, designs, and data produced for the Project shall be released under open licenses (Creative Commons or equivalent) consistent with the Project's open-knowledge principles.

### Article 5 — Financial Contributions and Liabilities

- Founders are not personally liable for Project debts or obligations beyond their own commitments.
- Initial financial contributions by Founders shall be documented and treated as either donations, loans, or capital contributions, as agreed in writing for each contribution.
- No Founder may unilaterally commit the Project to financial obligations exceeding ¥100,000 without consensus.

### Article 6 — Confidentiality

Founders agree to keep confidential:
- Sensitive partner negotiations
- Personnel matters
- Donor information not yet public
- Any other matters explicitly designated as confidential

This obligation continues for two years after a Founder's departure from the Project.

### Article 7 — Conflict of Interest

Founders shall disclose any conflicts of interest, including:
- Other organizational affiliations relevant to the Project's work
- Family or business relationships with Project partners or vendors
- Financial interests in companies the Project may engage with

Conflicted Founders shall recuse themselves from related decisions.

### Article 8 — Departure of a Founder

If a Founder wishes to leave the Project, they shall:
- Provide at least 60 days written notice where possible
- Cooperate in transition of responsibilities
- Return any Project materials in their possession
- Abide by the confidentiality provisions of Article 6

A departing Founder shall not be entitled to any financial compensation beyond what was previously agreed in writing.

### Article 9 — Removal of a Founder

A Founder may be removed from the founding group by unanimous vote of the remaining Founders, only in cases of:
- Serious breach of this agreement or the Founding Charter
- Conduct that materially harms the Project's reputation or mission
- Sustained inability or unwillingness to fulfill agreed responsibilities

A Founder facing removal shall have the opportunity to be heard before any vote.

### Article 10 — Transition to Formal Entity

The Founders agree that upon incorporation of the Project as a 一般社団法人 or other legal entity:
- This Agreement shall be replaced by formal bylaws (定款)
- Founders shall transfer any Project assets to the legal entity
- Each Founder shall be considered for, but not guaranteed, a role in the formal organization

### Article 11 — Dispute Resolution

Founders agree to resolve disputes in the following order:
1. Direct discussion between the Founders involved
2. Mediation by a mutually agreed third party (a respected advisor or community member)
3. Formal mediation through the Japan Arbitration Association or equivalent body
4. Legal action only as a last resort

### Article 12 — Governing Law

This agreement is governed by the laws of Japan. The agreement is drafted in both English and Japanese; in case of discrepancy, the **Japanese version shall prevail** for matters relating to interpretation under Japanese law.

### Article 13 — Amendments

This agreement may be amended only by unanimous written agreement of all current Founders.

## Appendix — Topics for Discussion Before Signing

These are common founder-agreement issues that benefit from explicit conversation **before** signing. We strongly recommend the founding group discusses each item:

1. **What if Rob has to leave Japan?** — succession planning
2. **What if a major funder requests changes** that conflict with the Charter?
3. **How do we handle media requests** — who speaks for the Project?
4. **What happens to Rob's existing relationships** (Safecast, YR-Design) — are these separate?
5. **What is the role of the village** in formal governance? Advisory? Veto power?
6. **How do we add new founders** later?
7. **What if Joi Ito or Ray Ozzie wishes to become more than an advisor?**
8. **At what stage do we start paying salaries**, and to whom first?
9. **What if a founder's personal financial situation changes** and they need to monetize their contribution?
10. **How do we handle disagreement** about the pace of public announcements?

These conversations are uncomfortable but vastly easier now than when the Project has money, partners, and public visibility.
